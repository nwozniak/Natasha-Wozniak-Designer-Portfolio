Huntré Equine
2019 Newsletter Redesign

Objective

Huntré Equine is an organization based in a fairly remote corner of NE India called Manipur, which also happens to be the place from which modern polo emerged. Despite the challenges of the the location, the organization has international support from polo organizations globally, and the newsletter needed to visually represent this global scope.

Starting from a text presentation, the objective was to create a visually rich newsletter that would compel various high level contacts to commit to fundraising and other types of support.

Huntré Equine
2019 Newsletter Redesign

Approach

Working with the founder of the organization L. Somi Roy, we focused on the key points and selected the appropriate photos from his collection to portray the story of ponies, Manipur and women’s polo.

For example, this infographic clearly represents the high proportion of polo players in Manipur vs. India overall.

Results

Brand Identity and Website Implementation

Objective

Create a brand identity and website for the non-profit film “Listen Closely”, which would allow the filmmakers to give more details about the project, the subjects, and the mission of the film, with a link to the donation page.

contact/ about

Brand Identity and Website Implementation

"Working with Natasha was a smooth and very positive experience. I needed a simple, professional site for my documentary film. She was responsive to my needs, helped me clarify what I *really* wanted, gave me great options and made changes quickly. I've received numerous compliments on the site and would happily work with Natasha again."

-Alana Sheeren

Approach

Without an extensive branding budget, we chose a font-based logo and a palette that complimented the color palette of the film. For ease of updates, a Squarespace template was used and the design was modified using the built-in styles as well as modification to the CSS.

Non-profit website

Website Design and Implementation

Objective

The Sangsangai website needed to convey the key achievements of the organization in the village of Rainaskot in order to serve as a fundraising tool for the organization.

This was accomplished through the use of the visually appealing imagery from the village and bold text blocks that convey the main points immediately. Once the viewer engages with the main story of the village, they can scroll down for more photos, a video and technical details about the homes.

contact/ about

Website Design and Implementation

Approach

The organization had a vast amount of detailed content, which had to be narrowed down to the key points of the project such as the location of the village, number of houses, and the outcome of the work there.

We collected all of the compelling imagery from the villages in Nepal, and used them to visually organize the themes and create a concise story for the website.

The website has an integrated donation page that collects donor information in a separate database as well as collecting payments.
